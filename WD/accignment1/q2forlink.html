Overview of bhagavad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita, often referred to as the Gita, is a Hindu scripture, dated to the second or first century BCE, which forms part of the epic poem Mahabharata.

The Gita is a synthesis of various strands of Indian religious thought, including the Vedic concept of dharma;

samkhya-based yoga and jnana; and bhakti.
